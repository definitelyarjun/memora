STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE — DAILY RESTAURANT OPERATIONS
Spice Garden Restaurant, Bangalore
Version 1.2 | January 2024

---

STEP 1 — Morning Stock Check
Actor: Kitchen Manager
Type: Manual
The kitchen manager arrives at 8:00 AM and physically checks all ingredient stocks against
a printed checklist. Quantities are written by hand into a paper logbook. If any item falls
below the reorder threshold, the manager writes it on a separate sticky note for the owner.
No digital system is used at this stage.

STEP 2 — Supplier Order Placement
Actor: Owner / Manager
Type: Manual
The owner reviews the sticky notes from the kitchen manager and calls each supplier
individually over WhatsApp or phone. Orders are verbal — no written purchase order is raised.
Delivery ETAs are noted mentally or on paper. Invoices arrive physically with the delivery
and are stored in a folder.

STEP 3 — Table Reservation Handling
Actor: Front-of-House Staff
Type: Manual
Customers call or walk in to make reservations. Staff record the reservation in a physical
diary with pen — name, time, party size. There is no central booking system. Double bookings
occasionally happen when multiple staff members take calls simultaneously.

STEP 4 — Order Taking at Table
Actor: Waiter
Type: Manual
Waiters take orders verbally using a handwritten notepad. The notepad is then walked to the
kitchen and pinned to a board. There is no KDS (Kitchen Display System) or POS tablet used.

STEP 5 — Kitchen Order Preparation
Actor: Chef / Kitchen Team
Type: Manual
Chefs read the handwritten slips and prepare dishes in order of arrival. No priority queue
or ticket timing system exists. Rush hours see orders getting mixed up or delayed.

STEP 6 — Billing and Payment
Actor: Cashier
Type: Manual (tool: basic calculator)
At the end of the meal, the cashier manually calculates the bill using a calculator and
writes the total on a printed blank receipt template. Payments are accepted as Cash or UPI
(PhonePe / Google Pay). No POS system is used. Daily totals are noted in a ledger book.

STEP 7 — Daily Sales Summary
Actor: Owner
Type: Manual (tool: Excel spreadsheet)
At closing time, the owner manually enters the day's total sales from the ledger into an
Excel spreadsheet on a laptop. This takes approximately 30-45 minutes each evening.
Staff salary calculations are also done manually in the same Excel file.

STEP 8 — Monthly Expense Reconciliation
Actor: Owner / External Accountant
Type: Manual (tool: Excel, paper invoices)
At month-end, the owner collects all paper invoices from the folder and hands them to an
external accountant who manually reconciles expenses in Tally. This process typically takes
2-3 days. GST filing is also handled manually by the accountant.
